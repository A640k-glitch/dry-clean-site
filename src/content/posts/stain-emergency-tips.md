The first few minutes after a spill often decide whether a stain will lift cleanly. Here is a practical order of operations.

## Step one—contain, do not scrub

**Blot** from the outside in with a dry, white cloth. Scrubbing pushes pigment deeper and can fuzz the surface. If the fabric is silk or wool, be especially gentle.

## Step two—know when to stop

Household remedies (salt, club soda, vinegar) sometimes help—and sometimes set a stain permanently. If you are unsure, **stop after blotting** and bring the garment in. Tell us what the stain is and what you already tried; it helps us choose the right treatment.

## Grease and oil

Absorbent powder can pull some oil from sturdy cottons, but delicates are better off in professional hands. We use solvents matched to the fiber and the stain age.

Ink and wine respond to different chemistry; age and heat-setting matter. Earlier is always better.
