The best storage strategy is simple: **nothing goes into a bin until it is clean.** Body oils and food traces attract pests and encourage odor even in a sealed container.

## Coats and jackets

After winter, bring wool and down pieces in for cleaning once you are done wearing them regularly. We check pockets, refresh linings, and return pieces ready for a breathable garment bag—not plastic long-term.

## Knits

Wash or clean **before** folding. Cedar blocks or lavender sachets help, but they do not replace cleaning. Store folded on a shelf; hanging heavy knits all summer can stretch shoulders.

## Spring and summer switch

When warm weather returns, shake out stored items in daylight, then air briefly before wearing. If a musty smell lingers, a light professional refresh beats masking it with fragrance.
