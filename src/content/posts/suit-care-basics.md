A good suit spends most of its life on a hanger or in motion—not crumpled in a corner. A few habits between visits to the cleaner make a noticeable difference.

## Rest and rotate

Give jackets **24 hours** on a wide, shaped hanger after a full day of wear. Wool recovers its drape when it can dry and relax. If you wear suits often, rotate between two so each piece gets a breather.

## Brush before bagging

Use a soft clothes brush on lapels and shoulders to lift dust and lint. It keeps soil from working into the fibers before your next **dry cleaning** cycle—which should stay occasional, not weekly, unless your job demands it.

## Deal with spills quickly

Blot—never rub—with a clean white cloth. For water rings, let the area air-dry fully; if a stain remains, bring the piece in and tell us what was spilled. The right solvent depends on the substance.

We press and finish suits with attention to lapels and pocket alignment so everything hangs the way the tailor intended.
