Delicate does not only mean “expensive.” It means fibers and construction that **do not tolerate** heat, agitation, or standard detergents the way cotton does.

## Silk and rayon blends

These can watermark, shrink, or lose sheen under the wrong process. We inspect seams and linings, then route pieces through programs meant for **low-agitation** cleaning and controlled finishing.

## Cashmere and wool knits

Machine washing—even on delicate—can **felt** wool or stretch ribbing. Dry cleaning at appropriate intervals removes body oils and odors while preserving shape. Always fold knits; do not hang heavy sweaters long-term.

## Beads and sequins

Heat from an iron or a too-hot press can melt adhesives. We use padding, lower temperatures, and hand finishing when trims are fragile.

When in doubt, point out trims at drop-off. We note it on your ticket.
